Supporting Canada’s communities to reach net zero by 2050

The mission of Sustainable Community Solutions Canada (SCS Canada) is to support communities in Canada to achieve net-zero goals by 2050 — with equity at the center.

Make net zero work for everyone — especially those left out.

We help North America’s communities cut emissions in buildings, transport, and waste while lowering bills and closing the energy burden gap (energy equity, not just energy tech). DEI isn’t a side quest when it comes to net zero — for us, it’s how we prioritize projects and measure success.

Turn climate plans into habits people actually adopt.

We co-create practical playbooks, run educational courses and public-awareness campaigns, and “teach the teachers” so residents, small businesses, and community members know what to do next week — not just by 2050. We create actionable roadmaps in collaboration with stakeholders and interest holders.

Build resilience you can feel when the lights flicker.

We back, research, and deliver minor, major, and deep retrofits, local renewables, and community resiliency programs. We focus on delivering emissions reduction, cost minimization, and closing the inequity gap.

Founding Members

Dr. Farzam Sepanta

Farzam holds a PhD in Building Engineering. He is a researcher in building decarbonization and an environmental, social, and governance (ESG) expert. With more than 20 published research articles and book chapters, his recent work focused on quantifying emissions associated with hybrid work for the Government of Canada. Farzam has also delivered courses on Architecture and Environment at Carleton University. His commitment goes beyond academia and research; he is a board member of Old Ottawa South Community Association (OSCA), has conducted multiple engagement programs (including equity and inclusivity in the workplace), and has been an active advocate for the community. At SCS Canada, Farzam leads the work on energy equity, programs, and community engagement.

Arya Parsaei

Arya is a building performance researcher focused on turning community climate plans into actionable programs. His work focuses on retrofits, M&V pipelines, and fault detection that improves comfort and lowers bills, which are a pillar in affordable and community housing. At SCS Canada, he leads the work on equitable retrofit, electrification, and GHG emissions.

Dr. Sharane Simon

Sharane is a building science and policy specialist whose work connects energy, behaviour, and resilient community infrastructure. She brings deep experience translating research on telework and building use patterns, retrofit pathways, and local renewables into plain language. At SCS Canada, her work focuses on policy and building resilient communities.
